value-based web caching despite traditional web caching techniques , redundant data is often transferred over http links . these redundant transfers result from both resource modification and aliasing . resource modification causes the data represented by a single uri to change ; often , in transferring the new data , some old data is retransmitted . aliasing , in contrast , occurs when the same data is named by multiple uris , often in the context of dynamic or advertising content . traditional web caching techniques index data by its name and thus often fail to recognize and take advantage of aliasing . despite traditional web caching techniques , redundant data is often transferred over http links . these redundant transfers result from both resource modification and aliasing . resource modification causes the data represented by a single uri to change ; often , in transferring the new data , some old data is retransmitted . aliasing , in contrast , occurs when the same data is named by multiple uris , often in the context of dynamic or advertising content . traditional web caching techniques index data by its name and thus often fail to recognize and take advantage of aliasing .
